— FAQ

Things firm owners ask before booking.

If your question isn't answered here, raise it on the discovery call. I'd rather know the hesitation up front.

Are you actually a practicing attorney, or just a tech person?
Both. I'm a licensed, practicing attorney with an active criminal & civil practice. I'm at the courthouse most weeks. I build software because I needed it for my own practice first.
Is this legal advice? Are you my lawyer?
No. This is business consulting — workflow, software, and operations — provided through a separate consulting LLC. I'm not your firm's outside counsel and we don't form an attorney-client relationship. If you need ethics opinions specific to your jurisdiction, I'll refer you to ethics counsel licensed where you practice.
Will my client data be safe?
Yes. Every system I build is designed for MRPC 1.6 confidentiality from the ground up. No client data leaves your environment unless you explicitly approve a vendor, and I'll always recommend self-hosted or enterprise-tier options when client material is involved. Mutual NDA before any audit work begins.
Do I need to be technical?
No. The whole point is that you don't. I work directly with your existing team — paralegals, intake, office manager — and leave behind systems they can run without writing a line of code.
What size firm do you typically work with?
Solo to roughly twenty-five attorneys. Larger firms have internal innovation teams; smaller firms get the most leverage from a single sharp engagement.
Do you take equity instead of cash?
Occasionally, for the right firm — typically a hybrid of reduced cash plus a small revenue share on the workflows we build together. Raise it on the discovery call.
